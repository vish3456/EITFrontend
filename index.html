Know Your Rights

The law is on
your side.

India has strong laws to protect you. This guide explains your rights in simple, clear language — no legal jargon, no judgment. You deserve to know what protection you can ask for.

ℹ️
This is an educational guide, not legal advice. For your specific situation, please speak with a legal aid lawyer. Free legal aid is available at NALSA — 15100 (National Legal Services Authority).
🏛️

Free Legal Aid — It's Your Right

Every woman can access free legal help in India. Call NALSA or visit your District Legal Services Authority.

Module 1 of 4
Domestic Harassment
Laws that protect you from cruelty, abuse, and harassment within a marriage or household.
IPC 498A
Protection from Cruelty by Husband
Emotional abuse, harassment for dowry, humiliation
›
What this law means
This law says that a husband or his family cannot treat a wife cruelly — whether through physical harm, emotional abuse, humiliation, or by demanding dowry (money/property). "Cruelty" includes name-calling, constant criticism, threats, isolating you from family, or any behaviour that damages your mental or physical health.
"You don't have to prove you were hit. Emotional pain and threats count under this law too."
⚡ What you can ask for
1
File an FIR at your nearest police station — the police must register a case. This is a cognisable offence, meaning police can act without a court warrant.
2
Arrest of the abuser — the husband and/or his family can be arrested under this law. The punishment is up to 3 years imprisonment.
3
Protection from further contact — along with the FIR, you can apply for a Protection Order under the DV Act so the abuser stays away from you.
📞 Women Helpline 181
DV Act 2005
Protection of Women from Domestic Violence
Comprehensive protection — home, money, children, safety
›
What this law means
This is one of India's most powerful laws for women. It covers ALL forms of domestic violence — physical, emotional, sexual, verbal, and economic — committed by any household member (husband, in-laws, relatives). Importantly, this law applies to live-in relationships too, not just marriages.
"Even if you live with him, this law can make him the one who has to leave — you have the right to your home."
⚡ What you can ask for
1
Protection Order — the court orders the abuser to stop all violence and have no contact with you.
2
Residence Order — you have the right to stay in your shared home. The abuser can be ordered to leave, even if the home is in his name.
3
Monetary Relief — claim money for medical bills, loss of earnings, and any harm caused by the abuse.
4
Custody Order — temporary custody of your children can be granted immediately to keep them safe.
5
Contact a Protection Officer — every district has a free government-appointed Protection Officer who helps you file the application at no cost.
📞 Women Helpline 181
IPC 306
Abetment of Suicide
When harassment drives a woman to harm herself
›
What this law means
If a person's harassment, threats, or cruelty drove you to attempt suicide or caused a loved one to end their life, that person can be criminally charged. The law recognises that emotional and psychological torture is a form of violence with serious consequences.
"If someone's behaviour made you feel like there was no way out — they are responsible. This law holds them accountable."
⚡ What you can ask for
1
File an FIR — the harassing person can be arrested and faces up to 10 years in prison.
2
Immediate safety — ask the magistrate for emergency protection if you fear further retaliation after speaking out.

Module 2 of 4
Physical & Sexual Assault
Laws that protect your body, your safety, and your dignity from physical harm and sexual violence.
IPC 304B
Dowry Death
When dowry demands lead to a woman's death
›
What this law means
If a woman dies due to burns, injury, or suspicious circumstances within 7 years of marriage, and there is evidence of dowry-related harassment before her death, it is treated as Dowry Death — a murder-level crime. This law exists because the court recognises the pattern of escalating dowry violence.
"If you are facing escalating pressure for dowry, please reach out now — before a crisis. The law is on your side."
⚡ What you can ask for
1
File an FIR immediately — family members of the accused can be arrested. The burden of proof shifts to them to prove innocence.
2
Magisterial inquiry — a special investigation is ordered for any suspicious death within 7 years of marriage.
3
For yourself now — combine with IPC 498A + DV Act to stop escalating harassment before it becomes fatal.
📞 Women Helpline 1091
IPC 323
Voluntarily Causing Hurt
Being hit, slapped, pushed or physically harmed
›
What this law means
If someone hits you, slaps you, pushes you, pulls your hair, or causes any physical pain intentionally — that is a criminal offence. You don't need to be severely injured for this law to apply. A slap is enough. It does not matter if the person is your husband, a family member, or a stranger.
"One slap is one assault. You do not have to wait until it gets worse. You have the right to report it now."
⚡ What you can ask for
1
File a police complaint / FIR — the abuser can be arrested and punished with up to 1 year in jail or a fine.
2
Medical examination — go to a government hospital immediately and ask for a Medico-Legal Certificate (MLC). This is key evidence.
3
Combine with DV Act — file for a Protection Order simultaneously to prevent further attacks.
📞 Call Police 112
IPC 354
Assault on Woman's Modesty
Unwanted touch, groping, forced contact
›
What this law means
This law protects you from any unwanted physical contact of a sexual nature — groping, inappropriate touching, being forcefully grabbed or hugged, or any assault intended to violate your dignity. It also covers actions like stalking (354D) and voyeurism (354C). You do not need to have been physically hurt for this crime to apply.
"Your discomfort and violation are valid. You don't need to minimise what happened to you for it to be taken seriously."
⚡ What you can ask for
1
File an FIR at the police station — police must register this case. The accused faces 1–5 years imprisonment.
2
Your identity is protected — under Indian law, your name cannot be published in media reports without your permission.
3
Report to a woman officer — you have the right to give your statement to a female police officer. You can insist on this.

Module 3 of 4
Economic & Living Rights
Laws that protect your right to financial support, a home, and a fair life.
Sec 125 CrPC
Right to Maintenance
Your right to financial support from your husband or family
›
What this law means
If your husband has abandoned you, refuses to support you financially, or you are unable to support yourself, the law requires him to pay you maintenance (regular money). This applies to wives, divorced women, and even live-in partners who have been deserted. The court decides a fair amount based on both your needs and his income.
"Financial control is abuse. You have the legal right to money for your basic needs — no matter what he says."
⚡ What you can ask for
1
Apply to the Magistrate's Court — file an application under Sec 125 for monthly maintenance. Courts can order interim (immediate) maintenance within days.
2
Interim maintenance — while the case is ongoing, you can receive temporary payments to cover your daily needs.
3
Arrears (backdated payment) — you can claim unpaid maintenance going back up to 1 year.
4
For your children too — this law also covers maintenance for your children if they are in your care.
📞 Free Legal Aid 15100
HMA
Hindu Marriage Act — Divorce & Alimony
Your right to leave and receive financial support after separation
›
What this law means
Under the Hindu Marriage Act, you have the right to file for divorce on grounds including cruelty, desertion (being abandoned for 2+ years), and domestic violence. After divorce, you are entitled to permanent alimony — regular financial support to maintain a lifestyle comparable to what you had during marriage. You also have equal rights over the matrimonial home.
"Leaving does not make you homeless or penniless. The law protects your right to a fair share and financial support."
⚡ What you can ask for
1
File for divorce in the Family Court on grounds of cruelty, desertion, or abuse — no mutual agreement needed.
2
Permanent alimony — ongoing monthly financial support after divorce based on his income and your needs.
3
Stridhan recovery — your jewellery, gifts, and personal property given before/during marriage are legally yours. You can sue to recover them.
4
Child custody and support — you can apply for sole or joint custody; the court will decide in the child's best interest.

Module 4 of 4
Fundamental Dignity
Constitutional rights that are yours simply because you are a person. These cannot be taken away — ever.
Article 14
Right to Equality
You are equal under the law — no exceptions
›
What this law means
The Constitution of India guarantees that every person — regardless of gender, religion, caste or background — is equal before the law and must be equally protected by the law. No institution, government official, or court can treat you as less deserving of justice because you are a woman.
"If any court, officer or institution treats you unfairly because you are a woman, they are violating the Constitution itself."
⚡ What you can ask for
1
File a Writ Petition in the High Court or Supreme Court if a law or official action discriminates against you based on gender.
2
Complain to the National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) if a government body denies you equal protection — call 14433.
3
Approach the National Commission for Women (NCW) — call 7827170170 to report gender-based discrimination by any authority.
📞 NCW Helpline
Article 15
Protection from Discrimination
No one can deny you rights because of your gender
›
What this law means
The State cannot discriminate against any citizen on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth. This means no government service — hospitals, courts, police stations, schools — can treat you differently or give you less access because you are a woman. Article 15(3) also allows the government to make special provisions specifically to help women, which is why women-specific laws and schemes exist.
"If a hospital refuses to treat you, a police officer refuses to file your complaint, or any official dismisses you — they may be violating your Constitutional right."
⚡ What you can ask for
1
Demand a Zero FIR — police cannot refuse to register an FIR for cognisable offences. If they refuse, file a complaint to the Superintendent of Police or the State Human Rights Commission.
2
Access to government schemes — you are entitled to all women welfare schemes, shelters (One Stop Centres — 181), and government legal aid.
📞 One Stop Centre 181
Article 21
Right to Life & Personal Liberty
Your right to live with dignity, freedom, and safety
›
What this law means
This is the most fundamental right in the Constitution. Article 21 says that no person shall be deprived of their life or personal liberty except by law. Courts have expanded this to mean the right to live with dignity — which includes the right to be free from violence, the right to a safe home, and the right to your own choices about your body and life. Domestic violence directly violates Article 21.
"Your right to a life of dignity is written into the Constitution. Anyone who takes that from you is violating India's highest law."
⚡ What you can ask for
1
Writ of Habeas Corpus — if you are being confined or held against your will in any home, a court can order your immediate release.
2
Public Interest Litigation (PIL) — if you or a group of women are systematically denied safety by any institution, you can file a PIL in the High Court.
3
Right to dignity in all proceedings — courts cannot question you in a degrading manner about past relationships. Object if they do, and note it on record.
